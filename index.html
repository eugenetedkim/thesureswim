How I approach pool care

I’ve been involved in pool service for most of my life, working alongside my dad as he ran his own pool business for nearly two decades. That experience shaped how I see pools — not as something to rush through, but as living systems that respond to care, consistency, and attention over time.

I started my own service to take that old-school foundation and apply it in a modern way. Better information, better chemistry practices, and more deliberate decisions — while keeping the hands-on mindset that made traditional pool service work.

The goal is clean, stable water that makes sense to maintain — chemically, mechanically, and practically.

Care, chemistry, and attention to detail

Pools are open bodies of water. Dust, dirt, pollen, and organic debris are constantly entering the pool — even when it looks clean. Algae doesn’t appear overnight; it starts quietly on surfaces long before the water changes color.

That’s why brushing and vacuuming matter. Physical removal breaks buildup early, especially after windstorms, rain, or heavy use. Weekly attention interrupts that process before neglect takes hold.

I pay close attention to stabilizer levels, chlorine behavior, pH balance, alkalinity, and calcium hardness — not as isolated numbers, but as a system that needs to stay predictable and easy to manage.

Cleaners should match the pool’s environment — debris type, surrounding trees, shape, and plumbing. Sometimes a simple setup works well; other times, a more capable option saves time and frustration. I focus on choosing what’s appropriate, not just what’s expensive.

I treat a pool like a body of water, not a decoration. Water changes quickly, equipment ages, and the environment never stops affecting it. The work is keeping it clean, fresh, and cared for — week after week.
